Setting Up a Blog Site: A Learning Journey in IT

As part of my transition into the IT field, I undertook a project to set up a blog site from scratch. This endeavor was not only a practical application of my burgeoning IT skills but also an opportunity to assist my girlfriend in launching her own blog. The process involved several key steps: domain registration, web hosting configuration, WordPress installation, email setup, and securing the site with SSL certification.

Domain Registration and Hosting

The first step was to purchase a domain through Namecheap, a reliable and cost-effective domain registrar. After acquiring the domain, I chose InfinityFree for hosting due to its no-cost service offer, making it an excellent option for starting out. Configuring the domain to use InfinityFree’s nameservers was a straightforward task, setting the stage for the subsequent steps.

WordPress Installation

Utilizing the Softaculous app installer available in the InfinityFree control panel, I installed WordPress. This platform was selected for its user-friendliness and robust community support, ideal for someone just starting their blogging journey. Although we initially faced DNS propagation delays that prevented immediate access to the WordPress admin panel, these were resolved within the typical 48-hour window.

Email Setup with ZohoMail

To professionalize communications, I configured ZohoMail for the domain’s email services. This involved setting up the necessary MX and other DNS records through Namecheap, ensuring all emails would be properly routed to Zoho’s mail servers.

SSL Certification

Securing the site was crucial, so I opted to use the free SSL certificate tool provided by InfinityFree. This required setting up a specific CNAME record for SSL verification—a process that taught me the intricacies of DNS management and the importance of accurate record configuration. Despite an initial hitch where the SSL status showed as "not found," adjustments to the DNS settings and ensuring correct propagation ultimately resolved the issue.

Challenges and Resolutions

Throughout this project, several challenges were encountered, primarily related to DNS settings and propagation delays. These challenges were instrumental in teaching me patience and the critical importance of meticulous configuration. Additionally, the inability to access the site directly via IP due to shared hosting limitations was an insightful introduction to web hosting nuances.

Learning Outcomes

This project was a valuable hands-on learning experience in several key areas of IT:

  • DNS Management: Understanding and configuring domain name systems.
  • Web Hosting: Setting up and managing web hosting services.
  • WordPress: Deploying and customizing content management systems.
  • Email Configuration: Integrating email services with a custom domain.
  • SSL Certification: Implementing security measures to protect site visitors.

By overcoming the technical challenges and successfully setting up the blog, I enhanced my practical IT skills, preparing me for further responsibilities in an IT role. This project has been a cornerstone of my IT learning journey, showcasing my ability to manage and execute web-based projects effectively.
