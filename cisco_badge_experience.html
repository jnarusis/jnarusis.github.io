Cisco Networking Basics: My First Badge

In this blog post, I’ll walk you through my journey of obtaining my first Cisco Networking Basics badge. This badge marked the beginning of my professional career in networking, and it introduced me to key concepts that laid the foundation for my future work in IT.

Introduction to Networking

Before pursuing this badge, I had only a basic understanding of how networks functioned. Through this course, I learned about the OSI model, TCP/IP protocols, routing, and switching. Each of these concepts played a critical role in shaping my understanding of how networks operate, from local environments to global-scale networks.

The Badge Process

The Cisco Networking Basics badge focused on hands-on labs and simulations, helping me to gain practical skills in configuring routers and switches. I worked on several exercises, including subnetting, VLAN setup, and basic routing configurations.

Lessons Learned

Achieving this badge taught me not only about networking but also about the discipline required to study, practice, and pass exams. It boosted my confidence and gave me a solid technical foundation that I continue to build on.

If you're just starting in networking, I highly recommend pursuing a badge like this to get a strong understanding of the basics.
